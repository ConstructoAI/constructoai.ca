Per-user fees: the part of Buildertrend pricing that surprises people

Buildertrend has three tiers in the United States as of 2026: Essentials at roughly $199 USD per month (plus per-user fees), Advanced at roughly $499 USD per month (plus per-user fees), and Complete at roughly $799 USD per month (plus per-user fees). Each tier has implementation fees on top, paid once. Per-user charges typically run $39-49 USD per seat per month depending on tier and role type (employee, sub, internal).

Constructo AI is $79.99 CAD flat per month for the entire company. Estimators, foremen, office admins, subcontractors with portal access, owners — all included. No setup fees, no implementation packages, no per-seat math to budget around.

Run the numbers for an 8-person remodeling shop on Buildertrend Advanced:

  • Buildertrend Advanced: $499/month base + 8 users × ~$49/user ≈ $891 USD/month ≈ $1,200 CAD/month at current exchange
  • Constructo AI: $79.99 CAD/month flat
  • Annual difference: roughly $13,500 CAD per year in software cost alone

For a 15-person custom home builder, the gap widens to roughly $18,000-22,000 CAD per year. That is a full-time bookkeeper's salary, or a year of leads, or a new truck — depending on how you think about the math. Constructo AI's pricing does not scale with headcount because we do not charge per seat. Add a foreman tomorrow. Same bill.

Honest scenarios: when each tool wins

Constructo AI wins

You are a Canadian remodeler or home builder bidding in English and French

If you build in Quebec, New Brunswick, eastern Ontario, or anywhere you send quotes to French-speaking homeowners, Buildertrend's English-only UI is a real obstacle. Estimates land in clients' inboxes in English. Crew checklists are English. Daily logs are English. Constructo AI flips between French and English with one click on every screen, and quotes render in the client's preferred language automatically. This alone can decide deals in bilingual markets.

Constructo AI wins

Your crew grew from 4 to 12 people in the last 18 months

This is where Buildertrend's per-user model hurts. Going from 4 to 12 users on Advanced takes you from roughly $695 USD per month to $1,087 USD per month — a $4,700 USD annual increase just because you hired people. Constructo AI stays at $79.99 CAD per month whether you have 4 employees or 50. Growth becomes a margin question, not a software-budget question.

Constructo AI wins

You do residential remodeling AND own a fabrication or trade specialty shop

A lot of contractors today are hybrid: a remodeling general contractor with an in-house cabinet shop, a roofer that fabricates flashings, an HVAC company with sheet metal production, an electrician with a panel-building shop. Buildertrend is purely residential GC software. Constructo AI includes a production module, work orders, BOM management, and operations tracking — the same platform handles your jobsite work and your shop work without bolting on a second SaaS subscription.

Buildertrend wins

Your business depends on real-time customer messaging with homeowners

Buildertrend's client portal with built-in customer messaging is genuinely best-in-class. If your renovation business model relies on a homeowner being able to chat with the project manager every day, see daily progress photos, and approve selections inside a single, polished interface, Buildertrend has invested years in that exact experience. Constructo AI's B2B portal covers status, approvals, and document sharing, but the in-thread messaging is not as developed.

Buildertrend wins

You run a US new-home development with mortgage and real estate integrations

If your business builds spec homes and your workflow involves close ties to US mortgage brokers, title companies, and real estate listing platforms, Buildertrend's US partner ecosystem has integrations Constructo AI does not offer. New home developers selling pre-construction units through US realtor networks will find Buildertrend's pipeline tools and partner referrals more directly relevant.

Buildertrend wins

Your sales team needs a heavily marketing-oriented lead capture flow

Buildertrend has invested significantly in marketing and lead-gen tooling — landing pages, web forms, sales pipeline analytics, drip campaigns. If your business spends substantial money on marketing-funnel optimization at the top of the funnel, Buildertrend's tools are more developed than Constructo AI's CRM, which is built for opportunity tracking and quote conversion rather than top-of-funnel lead capture.

All-You-Can-Eat versus a la carte: the real pricing philosophy

Buildertrend's pricing is a la carte. You pick a base plan, then you pay for every additional seat you add. Need a new estimator? Another $49 a month. Hire a project manager? Another $49. Give your favorite drywall sub portal access? Another $39. Need takeoff? That's an add-on. Want premium support? Another tier. Every business growth decision becomes a software cost decision.

Constructo AI is all-you-can-eat. One subscription. Unlimited employees. Every module included — estimating, takeoff, CRM, scheduling, daily logs, change orders, billing, payroll calculations, accounting integration, B2B portal, AI advisors. Hire your tenth foreman or your fiftieth. Add three new estimators. Give every sub portal access. Same bill: $79.99 CAD a month.

This is not a marketing slogan. It is a deliberate philosophical choice we made because contractors below 50 employees are not enterprise customers — they are real businesses with real margins and real volatility, and they should not have to call sales every time they hire someone.

The trade-off is real and honest: Buildertrend has been built specifically for residential remodelers for almost two decades, and that focus shows in their client portal, scheduling polish, and marketing tools. Constructo AI covers a broader range of contractors — residential, commercial, fabrication, trades — which means a few residential-specific features (mortgage referrals, deep customer messaging threading) are less developed. For most remodelers, the bilingual AI-driven flat-price platform wins. For the residential-customer-messaging power users, Buildertrend may still be the right pick.
