Constructo AI vs Jobber: Best Alternative for Contractors Doing Service + Projects (2026)

Jobber is the field service champion for trades doing short customer visits. But what if your business is half service calls and half real construction projects? Paying $349 to $599 USD per month for a tool that only solves the service half is a tough math. Here is an honest, side-by-side look.

TL;DR

Jobber is a Field Service Management (FSM) tool. Constructo AI is an FSM plus a Construction Management (CM) ERP in one. If your business is purely service (lawn care, pest control, cleaning, HVAC repairs only) with 1-3 people and high daily job volume, Jobber's mature dispatching and consumer booking widget probably wins. If you do any mix of service calls AND project work (renovations, new installs, multi-phase jobs, multi-trade coordination), Constructo AI handles both for $79.99 CAD/month flat instead of $349-599 USD/month per tier on Jobber.

Per-user tiers vs one flat price

Jobber publishes its prices openly, which is admirable. The 2026 USD lineup looks like this:

  • Core: $69 USD/month for 1 user
  • Connect: $169 USD/month for up to 5 users
  • Grow: $349 USD/month for up to 15 users
  • Plus: $599 USD/month for unlimited users (the only tier with the full feature set)

Annual billing knocks the prices down, but the structural issue remains: every time you hire, you risk jumping to the next tier. And the better features (route optimization, automation, marketing campaigns, two-way SMS) are gated behind higher tiers.

Constructo AI is $79.99 CAD flat per month for the entire company. Unlimited employees. Every module included. Hire 5 more electricians next month, pay the same. Add a project manager and an admin, pay the same.

For a typical 10-person electrical contractor doing both service calls and small project installs, the annual math runs:

  • Jobber Grow (max 15 users): $349 USD/mo × 12 = $4,188 USD/year (~$5,580 CAD)
  • Jobber Plus (unlimited): $599 USD/mo × 12 = $7,188 USD/year (~$9,580 CAD)
  • Constructo AI: $79.99 CAD/mo × 12 = $960 CAD/year

That is roughly $4,500 to $8,500 CAD/year saved, and that is before counting the construction modules Jobber simply does not offer (PDF takeoff, multi-phase projects, payroll, equipment rental, structural calculations).

Honest scenarios: when to pick which

Constructo AI wins

You do BOTH service calls AND construction projects

This is the most common Canadian contractor profile we meet. An electrical contractor with 8 trucks doing service calls during the week and one big basement rewire or new-build project rolling concurrently. A plumbing shop with 12 techs split between emergency calls and a multi-phase commercial bathroom retrofit. An HVAC contractor doing service contracts plus new-construction install jobs. Jobber handles the service half, but the project half falls off the table. Constructo AI handles both inside one client database, one schedule, one set of invoices.

Constructo AI wins

You are a multi-trade shop or you need payroll + takeoff + project management

If you employ multiple trades (carpenters, electricians, plumbers, drywallers under one roof) or if you need payroll built in, PDF plan takeoff for bidding, equipment rental tracking, and inventory across multiple sites, Jobber will require you to stitch together 3-4 separate subscriptions. Constructo AI has all of that in the flat $79.99 CAD/month.

Constructo AI wins

You serve clients in French as well as English

Quebec contractors, Acadian contractors in New Brunswick, eastern Ontario francophones, Manitoba St-Boniface area, northern Vermont and Maine. Jobber's interface and customer-facing emails are English-first, with limited French translation available. Constructo AI is natively bilingual with an instant toggle on every screen, and quotes/invoices/contracts can be sent in either language to the same client.

Jobber wins

You are a pure service trade with 1-3 people and 50+ jobs per day

If you run a lawn care, pest control, residential cleaning, snow removal, or pool service business where every visit is short, repeatable, and you do not do any construction or renovation work, Jobber's dispatching engine, route optimization, and recurring-job automation are genuinely best-in-class. The interface is purpose-built for this workflow and your team will be productive on day one.

Jobber wins

Your growth depends on direct online consumer bookings

Jobber's online booking widget is a true conversion machine for businesses that want a homeowner to land on their Google Business Profile or website and self-book a service appointment in 90 seconds without phone calls. The integration with Yelp, Google Reviews, and consumer trust signals is also more mature than what Constructo AI offers today. If your sales funnel is "Google search to booking widget to job," Jobber's polish is worth the price.

Jobber wins

High-volume dispatching: 50+ jobs per day across multiple crews

For a residential HVAC shop with 12 service techs running 8-10 calls each per day, or a multi-truck plumbing operation managing 80 dispatches daily, Jobber's route optimization and live map view have been refined over a decade. Constructo AI's scheduling handles project crews and milestone-based work well, but is not as battle-tested at 50+ daily dispatch volume.

Optimized for the truck, or optimized for the project?

Think of it this way. Jobber was designed for the truck. Every feature in Jobber assumes a tech is in a vehicle, on the way to a stop, doing a discrete piece of work, collecting payment, and moving to the next stop. That is the entire universe.

Constructo AI was designed for both the truck and the project. A project can last six weeks, involve five subcontractors, three deliveries, two inspections, four progress invoices, and a final hold-back. Or it can be a 45-minute service call. The platform handles both because Canadian contractors live both worlds.

Two tools or one? The honest answer is that running Jobber for service plus a separate construction tool (Buildertrend, Procore, CoConstruct) for projects works — but you end up paying for two subscriptions, training your team on two interfaces, duplicating your client database, and reconciling two sets of numbers into QuickBooks every month. That is real overhead.

Constructo AI bridges both because the underlying data model treats a "service call" and a "construction project" as the same object class with different lifespan settings. You sell hours and materials. You bill. You collect. The model does not care whether the job lasted 90 minutes or 90 days.

Where Jobber genuinely shines (we are not pretending otherwise)

Jobber has been around since 2011 and has roughly 200,000+ customers globally. They are based in Edmonton, Alberta. They know their corner of the market exceptionally well. Three things they do better than almost anyone:

  • Consumer-facing polish: Quote approval, online payment, automated reminders, the booking widget. The end-customer experience feels modern and frictionless.
  • Recurring service contracts: Recurring lawn care, pest control monthly visits, quarterly cleanings — Jobber's recurrence engine is mature and forgiving.
  • Canadian support: Edmonton-based, English-speaking, responsive. Constructo AI's support is also Canadian (Quebec-based) and bilingual, but Jobber has the larger team.

If you are a 1-3 person lawn care, cleaning, or pest control business and you will never quote a multi-phase construction project, signing up for Jobber is a defensible choice. We will say that without flinching.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Jobber for renovations and construction projects?

Jobber is built for field service work — short visits, individual jobs, route-based dispatching. It can technically handle small renovations, but it lacks construction-grade tools: no multi-phase project structure, no real Gantt chart, no work order hierarchy, no PDF takeoff for plans, no construction-grade job costing, no daily logs in the construction sense, no equipment rental tracking. Once a project lasts more than a couple of weeks or involves multiple trades and phases, Jobber starts breaking down. Constructo AI was designed from day one for that work.

Is Jobber suitable for new home builders or general contractors?

Not really. Jobber's core workflow is request to quote to job to invoice for a discrete service visit. New home construction involves dozens of subs, hundreds of line items, multiple inspection milestones, progress billing, change orders linked to the original contract, holdbacks, lien rights, deficiency lists, and structural calculations. None of that is in Jobber's DNA. Builders typically use Buildertrend, CoConstruct, or Constructo AI instead. For a homebuilder doing 5-30 starts per year, Constructo AI gives you the project management layer Jobber simply does not have.

How do I migrate from Jobber to Constructo AI?

Jobber exports clients, jobs, quotes, invoices, and time entries to CSV. Constructo AI imports those CSVs into the equivalent modules (clients, opportunities, devis, invoices, time tracking). Recurring jobs need to be reconfigured because Jobber's recurrence engine differs from Constructo AI's contract module. QuickBooks sync stays continuous through the QuickBooks integration on both sides. Typical migration takes 6-12 hours of work for a 5-15 person service business. Email info@constructoai.ca for a free guided migration call.

Can I use Jobber and Constructo AI together?

Some contractors run both during a transition period — Jobber for legacy recurring service contracts and Constructo AI for new project work. The two do not have a direct integration today, so you would manage clients in both places and reconcile billing in QuickBooks. We do not recommend long-term parallel operation because it doubles your admin work and confuses the team. The cleaner approach is to consolidate everything in Constructo AI within 60-90 days once your team is trained.

Why is Jobber so much more expensive at scale than Constructo AI?

Jobber uses tiered per-user pricing: Core at $69 USD/month for 1 user, Connect at $169 USD/month for 5 users, Grow at $349 USD/month for 15 users, and Plus at $599 USD/month for unlimited users. As you add field crew, dispatchers, and office staff, you jump tiers fast. Constructo AI charges a flat $79.99 CAD/month for the entire company regardless of headcount. A 10-person electrical contractor pays roughly $80 CAD/month on Constructo AI vs $349-599 USD/month on Jobber — a 5x to 10x annual savings, before currency conversion.

Does Constructo AI offer the same online booking widget as Jobber?

Not in the same form. Jobber has one of the best consumer-facing online booking widgets in the market, designed for homeowners to self-book a service appointment from your website or Google profile. Constructo AI focuses on B2B and B2C quoting workflows through its client portal — a logged-in client space where customers approve quotes, sign contracts, pay deposits, view project progress, and message you. If your entire growth engine is direct-from-Google bookings for short residential service calls, Jobber's widget is more polished. If your sales cycle involves estimates, site visits, and contracts, Constructo AI's portal fits better.
